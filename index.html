02 · M.S. Thesis · Aug 2022

Snow Water Equivalent Predictions in Utah

Problem: National snow water models struggle with Utah's mountainous terrain, making water availability predictions unreliable for a desert state that depends on snowmelt.

Approach: Created an R package (rsnodas) that lets users access national gridded data (PRISM and SNODAS), build their own models, and combine predictions with SNOTEL station data using an ensemble weighting method.

Result: An open-source tool that enables researchers to explore and improve SWE predictions across Utah and similar terrain.

Key takeaway: The ensemble of national gridded data and GAMs showed marginal improvements in median and SD of errors — honest results that highlight where future research should focus.

Approach

How I Work with Data

Every project starts with a question and ends with a clear answer someone can act on.

01

Ask the Right Question

In plain English, What are the questions we trying to answer? For example: How can we use game footage to help pickleball players understand and improve their game?

02

Understand the Data

How was the data collected? What assumptions come with it? Explore each column, create visuals to spot issues, and understand what's trustworthy before modeling.

03

Model & Validate

Fit the right model for the question — not the fanciest one. Check assumptions, evaluate performance, and ask: does this actually help answer the original question?

04

Communicate Clearly

What visuals and language help someone who isn't a statistician understand the findings? The insight only matters if people can act on it.
